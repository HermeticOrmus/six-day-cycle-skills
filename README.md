> **A single `CLAUDE.md` for sustainable shipping cadence. Six days of work mapped to Hermetic principles, mandatory rest, no 7-day weeks. Burnout-aware development rhythm.**

## Why

The standard tech-industry advice — ship faster, optimize the funnel, sprint to launch — produces shippers who burn out at 18-month intervals and codebases that calcify because polish always loses to the next feature.

The 6-Day Development Cycle is the opposite discipline: bounded work, intentional phases, sacred rest. It scales from a single feature (one 6-day cycle) to a multi-quarter program (consecutive cycles with explicit rest between).

## The cycle

| Day | Focus | Principle |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Ideate & Research | Mentalism |
| 2 | Design & Plan | Correspondence |
| 3-4 | Build | Vibration |
| 5 | Test & Polish | Cause-Effect |
| 6 | Launch | Gender |
| Rest | Integrate, celebrate, prepare | Rhythm |

The principle mapping comes from the Hermetic tradition (see [`hermetic-laws-skills`](https://github.com/HermeticOrmus/hermetic-laws-skills)). You don't have to buy into the philosophy to use the cadence — the day-shape works on its own.

Full content: [`CLAUDE.md`](CLAUDE.md). Worked example: [`EXAMPLES.md`](EXAMPLES.md).

## Scaling the cycle

- **1-hour cycle**: 5 ideate / 5 plan / 30 build / 10 test / 10 launch / break
- **3-day cycle**: Ideate+Design / Build+Test / Polish+Launch / rest
- **6-day cycle**: as above
- **2-week cycle**: 2 days each phase / 2 days rest
- **Multi-cycle programs**: consecutive cycles with explicit rest between

The day-shape scales; rest does not get smaller proportionally.

## What this isn't

- **A productivity hack**. The point is sustainability, not output maximization.
- **Anti-deadline**. Deadlines and rhythm coexist. The cycle is how you hit deadlines without breaking.
- **A magic schedule**. Pick whatever rhythm fits your context. The principle is: bounded work, mandatory rest.
